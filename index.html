About d-AI-s Days

🧠 Real Artificial Intelligence at EAFIT University

Welcome to d-AI-s Days, an event dedicated to real artificial intelligence — created by and for passionate and competitive innovators. Hosted at EAFIT University, this is our first edition, and we're setting our sights high: to become the premier AI and programming event in Colombia, surpassing even PyCon Colombia as a national reference for technology and innovation.

🌟 Our Vision

We are building something that grows one day at a time — literally. Each year, we plan to add one more day to the event, reaching a total of six days of world-class content, learning, and networking.

📈 2025 Edition

  • Expected attendees: 70–90 participants
  • Location: EAFIT University, Medellín, Colombia
  • Focus: Real-world applications of Artificial Intelligence
  • Organizers: Machine Learning EAFIT & Medellín AI

🌍 Future Plans

In the coming years, we aim to:

  • Expand to two auditoriums
  • Attract national and international speakers
  • Secure sponsorships
  • Include panel discussions and open debates

🎤 Open to the Public

This event is open to everyone. You can even become a speaker next year — all you need is to attend the training sessions provided by our event trainees.

🏆 Speaker Challenge

During the event, speakers will compete to deliver the best talk of the conference. The first, second, and third place winners will receive special recognition for their outstanding presentations. This friendly competition encourages creativity, clarity, and passion in every talk.

Conference Schedule

Time Slot Speaker Title Description
8:00 - 8:30 Register the attends Register the attends for the event.

9:00 - 9:45 Juan David Martinez Topic: Large Language Models (LLMs) This talk will delve into the world of Large Language Models (LLMs).
9:45 - 10:30 Manuela Larrea Pixels, Priors & Paranoia: From Adversarial Competition to Collaborative ML The talk will explain GANs, their evolution, and how CV is integrating with LLMs through Diffusion Models and Transformers.
10:30 - 11:00 Break Morning break.
11:00 - 11:45 Lucas Barbier Orchestrating Complex Development Processes with AI Agents we’re going to dive deep into how to orchestrate complex development processes using spec-driven development with the help of Claude Code Opus 4.1. We’ll show how to design new features and roadmaps that truly align with what a project needs, incorporating methodologies like Vibe Kanban to intelligently distribute tasks among the mini-orchestrators — Claude’s sub-agents — and build a complete workflow without losing control of the context window. We’ll also discuss the initial setup, which starts with defining Changlof Rules and configuring Claude Files — the key components that help agents understand documentation, requirements, and the overall work plan. From there, agents can fully leverage tools and MCPs, improving testing and maintaining the entire project flow. Finally, we’ll talk about the importance of creating worktrees and templates to industrialize the development process. We’ll explain how this approach produces much more deterministic responses, thanks to prompt engineering tuning by agents, turning weak prompts into clear, powerful, and useful instructions, ensuring consistent and easy-to-review results.
11:45 - 12:30 Jorge Ivan Padilla Topic: To be announced
12:30 - 14:00 Lunch Lunch break.
14:00 - 14:45 Santiago Cartagena Reinforcement Learning in Action: How MercadoLibre Optimizes Its Advertising with Multi-Armed Bandits We’ll explore how MercadoLibre uses Reinforcement Learning techniques to optimize the daily selection of advertising creatives. Through strategies such as Thompson Sampling and epsilon-greedy, the goal is to balance exploration of new ads with the exploitation of the most effective ones, maximizing the CTR (Click-Through Rate) on the website’s banners. We’ll discuss the practical challenges of implementing multi-armed bandits at scale, from defining the environment to offline and online evaluation, and how this technology drives personalization in the user experience.
14:45 - 15:30 Santiago Lopez AlphaFold: Using AI to Find Cures for Diseases The talk will explain the functioning of Google's AlphaFold models, their evolution, and their relevance.
15:30 - 16:00 Break Afternoon break.
16:00 - 16:45 Cesar Gonzales (Special Guest) Get Your Ducks in a Row, Then Hit: World Models to Define a Strategy In the real world, there aren't many opportunities to make mistakes. Failing can cost an opportunity, not yield the expected result, or come at a high price.   Humans have developed a process to be more effective: We understand the situation and its environment, focus on what's relevant, imagine, practice, and experiment to finally act with the available information. And if we still fail, we learn why and make corrections for the next time.   World Models is an AI tool capable of building an understanding of its world to practice, predict, and act in a much more assertive and safer manner, define an strategy (Get your ducks in a row), and then move (hit).
16:45 - 17:00 Closing Closing session for the event.
